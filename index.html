Bio

Bahar completed her B.Sc. in Electrical Engineering from the K. N Toosi University of Technology (2006). For her first M.Sc. in Biomedical Engineering at Tehran Polytechnic (2010), she used artificial neural networks to model neural correlates of the motor cortex and brain stem involved in speech production. In 2012, she moved to Lincoln, Nebraska to pursue a second M.Sc. in Computer Science (2018) and a Ph.D in Communication Disorders (2018) from the University of Nebraska. During this time, she worked on recognizing emotions in human speech using convolutional neural networks, as well as on understanding the interaction between temporal & spectral characteristics of acoustic signals in relation to speech intelligibility.

In 2018 she joined the Neuroscience Program at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign. Working at the Audiory & Cognitive Neuroscience Laboratory, she used brain imaging techniques along with machine learning to understand and characterize neural correlates of the tinnitus condition. At her time in Illinois she has also served as a graduate lecturer for her department. Since 2020, she has been a postdoctoral researcher with Columbia University as well as the National Institutes of Mental Health.

In her free time, she enjoys reading, traveling and playing the piano. She occasionally records songs with her husband.
